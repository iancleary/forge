# Business Diagrams

This reference is for startup leadership, product, operations, GTM, finance, and planning communication.

## `journey`

Use for:

- customer lifecycle
- onboarding friction
- support escalation paths
- marketplace seller/buyer experience

Why:

- it communicates stages and sentiment better than a plain flowchart

## `gantt`

Use for:

- roadmap windows
- launch plans
- fundraise or hiring plans
- multi-stream delivery timelines

Why:

- explicit time is the point

## `timeline`

Use for:

- company milestones
- incident history
- product evolution narrative

Why:

- it is lighter than Gantt when dependencies are not the story

## `kanban`

Use for:

- work-state communication
- intake vs in-flight vs blocked vs done
- ops or growth experiment boards

Why:

- status is easier to scan than task chronology

## `quadrantChart`

Use for:

- prioritization matrices
- market positioning
- effort vs impact
- risk vs return

Why:

- it is the fastest way to visualize tradeoff space

## `pie`

Use for:

- a small number of share-of-whole categories

Avoid when:

- the audience needs precise comparisons or there are many slices

## `sankey-beta`

Use for:

- funnel dropoff
- budget flow
- traffic sources to conversion destinations
- workload split across teams

Why:

- flow magnitude is the message

## `xychart-beta`

Use for:

- growth trends
- revenue vs cost
- retention curves
- experiment results over time

Why:

- it handles numeric comparison better than `pie` or `quadrantChart`

## `radar-beta`

Use for:

- vendor comparison
- team capability scoring
- feature-completeness comparisons

Why:

- it works when several dimensions matter at once and exact numeric decoding is secondary

## `treemap-beta`

Use for:

- portfolio allocation
- revenue mix
- cloud cost decomposition
- feature usage share

Why:

- many categories stay readable while preserving part-to-whole structure

## `mindmap`

Use for:

- strategy decomposition
- board prep
- launch-planning brainstorms
- taxonomy framing before a more formal diagram

## `requirementDiagram`

Use for:

- product or compliance requirements with validation method
- linking requirements to implementation elements

Why:

- traceability is built into the syntax

## `venn`

Use for:

- overlapping customer segments
- feature-set overlap
- market intersections

## `ishikawa`

Use for:

- root-cause analysis of churn, incidents, missed launches, or failed experiments

## Recommended business bundle

For most startup writeups, the high-value set is:

- `journey`
- `quadrantChart`
- `gantt`
- `timeline`
- `kanban`
- `sankey-beta`
- `xychart-beta`
- `treemap-beta`
- `requirementDiagram`
